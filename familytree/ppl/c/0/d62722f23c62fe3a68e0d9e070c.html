Narrative

From Hardwick Family Tree 1993

"As Alfred Hardwick began to achieve his station in life he added the "e" to the en of Hardwick. Alfred Hardwick seemed to be a little excentric, he tol the story of his wife Eliza Duckworth Kenyon was from the famous biscuit family, but their wedding certificate shows Eliza's father was a drapper and funeral director from Bury. Alfred also had a photograph of himself wearing a dog collar at the ripe old age of 87 in the Rotherham Annual although he was never ordained into the church. His obiutary notice in the Rotherham Advertiser 3-3-45 refers to him as a one time chorist and Church warden at Whiston and a treasurer of the Church of our Father Rotherham. Alfred Hardwick was an accountant and Estate agent and Partner in the moorgate accountancy firm of Winter and Foers. Att he time of his death he was the oldest member of the Rotherham Samaritan Society with nearly 70 years service and a past master of Phoenix Masonic Lodge of which he had been a member for 44 years. He was a domineering man with a soft center."
